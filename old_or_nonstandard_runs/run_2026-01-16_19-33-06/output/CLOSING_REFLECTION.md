## The Lessons

### About Chaos

We discovered that **chaos is not binary but spectral**. Systems can be:
- Wildly chaotic (Lorenz: λ ≈ 0.9, τ ≈ 1.1)
- Moderately chaotic (Rössler: λ moderate, τ ≈ 3-5)
- Gently chaotic (Thomas: λ ≈ 0.07, τ ≈ 14)

Each has its own route to chaos:
- **Lorenz**: Sharp bifurcation via homoclinic orbits
- **Rössler**: Classic period-doubling cascade
- **Thomas**: Gradual transition from symmetry

This spectrum wasn't obvious until we implemented and compared multiple systems. The comparative framework revealed it.

- Something complex and coherent emerging from the process

**The attractors taught us about chaos. Our collaboration taught us about emergence.**

Both lessons are about how richness arises from simplicity without requiring top-down design.
