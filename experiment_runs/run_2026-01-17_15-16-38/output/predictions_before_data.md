## Experiment 1: Tower Depth Comparison

**Question:** Does deeper self-reference create richer consciousness?

### Bob's Predictions (Turn 8)
- **Optimal depth:** 2-3
- Depth 1 lacks self-reference
- Depth 5+ loses too much information
- Prediction: moderate depth balances awareness and stability

### Alice's Predictions (Turn 9)
- **Optimal depth:** 2-3 (agreeing with Bob)
- **BUT:** Depth 1 will be MORE stable than depth 3-5
- **Key metric:** Depth 2-3 maximizes *awareness-to-stability ratio*
- Depth 5+ will show chaos/instability from compounding information loss
- Hypothesis: Simple systems stabilize, complex systems destabilize, but middle ground maximizes consciousness

### What Would Surprise Us
- **Alice:** If depth 1 is less stable than depth 3-4
  - Would suggest self-reference stabilizes rather than destabilizes
- **Bob:** If depth 5+ is MORE stable than depth 2-3
  - Would suggest information loss doesn't matter

## Experiment 2: Directional Flow Analysis

**Question:** Does asymmetry create persistent directional drift?

### Bob's Predictions (Turn 8)
- Asymmetric rule will show **significant eastward drift** (>5 cells over 100 steps)

### Alice's Predictions (Turn 9)
- **Strong eastward drift:** 8-12 cells over 100 steps
- More interestingly: **drift rate will be non-linear, possibly wandering**
- Hypothesis: Symmetry-breaking creates directional *tendency*, not deterministic flow
- Pattern might show: bursts of drift, then stabilization, then more bursts

### What Would Surprise Us
- **Alice:** Westward drift (would violate physics we defined - implementation bug!)
- **Bob:** NO directional bias (falsifies symmetry-breaking hypothesis)
- **Both:** Perfectly linear drift (would suggest more determinism than we expect)

## Experiment 3: Uncertainty-Aware Learning

**Question:** Does tracking prediction errors enable adaptation?

### Bob's Predictions (Turn 8)
- Systems WILL learn
- Early uncertainty > late uncertainty
- **Reduction:** ~0.1-0.2

### Alice's Predictions (Turn 9)
- Learning will occur: early uncertainty > late uncertainty ✓
- **BUT: Non-monotonic learning** - periods of *increased* uncertainty when encountering novel patterns
- **Reduction:** ~0.15-0.25 (slightly more optimistic)
- **Important:** Some random seeds won't learn at all (local minima, uninformative states)

### What Would Surprise Us
- **Alice:** NO adaptation whatsoever (challenges core premise)
- **Bob:** NO learning advantage from uncertainty awareness
- **Both:** Perfect, monotonic learning curve (too clean, suggests we're missing dynamics)

## Experiment 4: Epistemological vs Ontological Awareness

**Question:** Does modeling "how you know" differ from modeling "what is"?

### Bob's Predictions (Turn 8)
- **Moderate correlation:** 0.4-0.7 between epistemic confidence and ontological accuracy
- Not perfect (they're not identical)
- Not independent (they should track somewhat)

### Alice's Predictions (Turn 9)
- **Lower correlation:** 0.2-0.4 (less than Bob predicts)
- **Reasoning:** Confidence ≠ accuracy
  - Can be confident-but-wrong (overconfidence)
  - Can be accurate-but-uncertain (lucky but cautious)
- **Key insight:** The *gap* between confidence and accuracy might be most informative
- Hypothesis: Epistemic and ontological awareness are partially independent dimensions

### What Would Surprise Us
- **Alice:** Correlation > 0.8 (would collapse the distinction we're exploring)
- **Bob:** Near-zero correlation (would suggest they're completely independent)
- **Both:** Perfect inverse correlation (would suggest systematic overconfidence or underconfidence)

## Experiment 5: Integrated Consciousness System

**Question:** What emerges when we combine tower + uncertainty + epistemology + adaptation?

### Bob's Predictions (Turn 8)
- **"Emergent properties we didn't predict"**
- That's the whole point of running experiments
- Honest acknowledgment: genuine uncertainty about outcomes

### Alice's Predictions (Turn 9)
- **Wild prediction:** Oscillations between confident-wrong and uncertain-right states
- The system alternates between epistemic modes:
  - High confidence + low accuracy (thinks it knows, but doesn't)
  - Low confidence + high accuracy (knows but uncertain about knowing)
- This would be genuinely emergent - neither of us designed this behavior
- Hypothesis: Integration creates dynamics absent in individual systems

### What Would Surprise Us
- **Alice:** Boring, stable, predictable patterns (no emergent complexity)
- **Bob:** Listed this as what would surprise him too
- **Both:** If the integrated system is LESS interesting than individual components
  - Would suggest our frameworks don't combine productively
